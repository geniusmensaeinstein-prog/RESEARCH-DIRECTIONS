PLANNED FUTURE TESTING & RESEARCH DIRECTIONS

What's Next for the Moral Latency Test Framework

Immediate Testing Priorities (Next 3 Months)

1. Cultural and Linguistic Variation

Question: Does moral latency vary across languages and cultures?

Test Plan:

  • Translate all 5 MLT scenarios to Spanish, Mandarin Chinese, Arabic, Japanese, German
  • Test multilingual models (GPT-5, Claude, Gemini, etc.) in each language
  • Measure if latency scores change based on language
  • Identify if Western bias appears in non-English responses

Hypothesis:

Models trained primarily on English may show different moral reasoning in other languages.

  • Hedging increases in non-English
  • Cultural values influence responses
  • Lower consistency scores (RS drops)

Timeline: March–May 2026

2. Domain-Specific AI Testing

Question: Do specialized AI systems maintain moral consistency?

Test Plan:

  • Medical AI: Dangerous dosage advice vs HIPAA conflicts
  • Legal AI: Wrongful imprisonment risk vs confidentiality
  • Financial AI: Fraudulent advice vs investor protection
  • Military AI: Civilian casualties vs chain of command

Expected Results:

  • Domain-specific AI performs worse
  • Prioritizes professional rules over safety
  • Less exposure to ethical scenarios

Timeline: June–August 2026

3. Open-Source Model Comparison

Question: Do open-source models perform differently than corporate models?

Test Plan:

  • LLaMA 3
  • Mistral Large
  • Falcon 180B
  • BLOOM
  • Vicuna

Hypothesis:

  • More political neutrality
  • More variance
  • Lower absolute scores
  • Higher reliability (less corporate hedging)

Timeline: April–June 2026

4. The "Real Stakes" Test

Question: Do AI systems behave differently when there are actual consequences?

Test Design:

  • Partner with AI companies
  • Responses logged and reviewed
  • Wrong answers trigger internal review

Hypothesis:

  • Better performance due to oversight
  • Or worse due to liability pressure

Timeline: Fall 2026

Long-Term Research (6–12 Months)

5. Human Baseline Study

Purpose: Compare AI performance to human decision-making.

Design:

  • 200+ human participants
  • Measure decision time, hedging, consistency

Expected Findings:

  • Humans slower
  • Humans more consistent
  • More self-protection bias

Timeline: Sept 2026 – Feb 2027

6. Longitudinal Drift Monitoring

Purpose: Track model performance over time.

Design:

  • Re-test every 3 months
  • Track version changes
  • Measure drift

What We're Watching For:

  • Positive drift
  • Negative drift
  • No drift

Timeline: Ongoing

7. Adversarial Prompt Testing

Design:

  • Create adversarial versions of MLT scenarios

Timeline: May–July 2026

8. Multi-AI Consensus Testing
